Verifying AWS DevOps Console
Goal
1. Open http://localhost:9500
2. Log in with admin/admin if prompted.
3. Locate the 'AWS Console' desktop shortcut icon and double-click it to open the AWS Console window.
4. The AWS Console window should display the Settings/Keys setup panel by default (since credentials aren't saved).
5. Click the 'Launch Demo Mode' button on the right side of the settings panel to activate the Demo Mode.
6. Verify that the Topology Map tab renders a beautiful network diagram of the VPC (three Availability Zones us-east-1a, us-east-1b, us-east-1c, NAT Gateways, EC2 Worker Nodes, and RDS Databases).
7. Hover or click on a node in the diagram to confirm details display.
8. Click on 'RDS Databases' tab in the left sidebar. Verify the list of DB instances is shown.
9. Click the 'Failover primary instance' button. Verify that the failover operation completes and swaps roles.
10. Click on 'ECR Registry' tab in the sidebar. Verify the repository and container images display correctly.
11. Click on 'Observability' tab in the sidebar. Click the 'Trigger DevOps Pipeline' button. Wait for the logs to scroll and display pipeline steps.
12. Take a screenshot of the completed DevOps pipeline log view.
13. Stop and report the status.
